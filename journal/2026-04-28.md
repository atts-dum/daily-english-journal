## Reading and Audible

### Original 🤔
Lately, I’ve been listening to Audible while working.  
In 10 days, I finished listening to four titles—which normally take 50 hours—at double speed.  
The advantage of Audible is that it takes less time than reading.  
But I get the feeling it won’t stick with me.  
Above all, the problem is that it’s such an interesting work that I just want to read it.  

### Corrected 🤖
Lately, I've been listening to Audible while I work.  
In just 10 days, I finished listening to four books at double speed. Usually, it would take me 50 hours to listen to all four books.  
The advantage of Audible is that it takes less time than reading.  
But I don't remember the content well.  
The main problem is that when a book is interesting, I end up wanting to read it myself.  

### Memo (dum!)
集中力のないボクにとって、50時間のリスニングは500時間のリーディングに相当する　つまりこの先本を手にすることはないということ  
🤖 For someone like me who has trouble staying focused, 50 hours of listening is equivalent to 500 hours of reading. In other words, I probably won’t ever pick up these books again.
