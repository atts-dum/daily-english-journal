## The Law of “inertia(or force of habit)”

### Original 🤔
Since the interpretations differ slightly between English and Japanese, I’ll define “inertia” here: it refers to a long-standing habit.  
This is the kind of delivery that, while boring, requires a certain level of focus.  
Unintentionally going to a delivery address even though the subscription has been canceled.  
I call this the law of inertia. Of course, it's different from “Newton's first law.”

### Corrected 🤖
Since the meaning of the word “inertia” differs slightly between English and Japanese, I’ll explain it here. It refers to a habit that has persisted for many years.  
This applies to deliveries, which, while boring, do require a certain degree of concentration.  
I find myself unconsciously heading to delivery locations where the service has been canceled.  
I call this the “law of inertia.” This is different from “Newton’s First Law.”
